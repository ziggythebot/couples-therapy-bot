# Safety & product boundaries (beta)

## What this is

Relationship pattern discovery tooling—not therapy, crisis care, legal advice, or emergency services.

## Boundaries

The beta may:

- Summarize communication patterns from provided text
- Surface conflict/repair-style signals in a descriptive way
- Suggest light, optional conversation prompts or experiments

The beta must not:

- Diagnose mental health conditions
- Assign blame or make legal judgments
- Tell anyone to stay in or leave a relationship
- Present itself as a substitute for crisis lines or professional care

## User-facing disclaimer

Use clear copy wherever the product is introduced—for example:

> I may be missing important context, and I’m not a crisis service. If there is immediate danger, contact local emergency services. If you feel unsafe in your relationship, reach out to a qualified professional or a trusted local hotline.

## Output style (briefs and summaries)

- Non-diagnostic wording; separate observations from inferences
- Prefer evidence-backed claims; avoid absolutes unless quoted
- Optional confidence labels (`low` / `medium` / `high`) on major patterns

## Data handling (beta)

- Store only what you need for analysis; prefer anonymized samples for testing
- Keep artifacts local by default; delete a couple’s folder to remove their data

## Later (post-beta)

Automated risk detection, blocking flows, and clinical oversight hooks are out of scope for this beta build; revisit before wider or regulated use.
